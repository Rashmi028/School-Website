ST. KAREN'S SECONDARY SCHOOL BRIEF HISTORY
HOW ARE WE HERE

St. Karen's Secondary School was founded
in 1990 by Mr. and Mrs. D.P. Galstaun
The Patron Saint of the school is St. Katherine of Siena . Her feast day is on the 29th April.
This is celebrated as St. Karen's day. It derived its name from the novel KAREN written by
Marie Lyons Killilea. 'KAREN' is the inspiring true story of a little girl who triumphed over
disaster and the delightful family who made her triumph possible. Imbued with the same
spirit of sacrifice,love,devotion and care for children, St. Karen's Secondary School was
established. St. Karen's Secondary school is a minority Anglo-Indian Christian Institution,
by reason of both religion and language. It is managed by the St. Karen's Anglo-Indian Education
Society which is a registered body under the Society act. This Society was established solely for
the education and cultural development primarily of Anglo- Indian Christian children seeking the
same and admitting inter-alia such other children irrespective of caste, creed, location, language,
sex or religion as are desirous of recieving education in St. Karen's Secondary School.
